# Lesson 2 audit — 2026_1RE_border_art

## Verdict

Lesson 2 is validable with minor implementation safeguards.

The lesson is coherent with the sequence architecture and the support lock. It uses the reduced `Timeline of border storytelling` as a micro-support, not as a full historical lesson. Its main contribution is useful: pupils move from “the border as a wall” to “the border as a story of control, movement and human lives.”

The lesson may be implemented before creating a worksheet, provided that the timeline remains strictly reduced and the activity does not become a date-memorisation task.

## 1. Alignment with the final task

### Diagnosis

The alignment is satisfactory.

Lesson 2 prepares a necessary contextual sentence for the final curatorial pitch:

> The U.S.-Mexico border is not only a wall. It also tells a story of control, movement and human lives.

This is directly useful for the final task because pupils will later need to explain why artists react to the border and why an exhibition about Border Art is culturally meaningful.

### Strength

The lesson avoids turning the context into background knowledge for its own sake. It links the timeline to the future pitch through the idea:

> artists react to a border story, not to an empty wall.

### Risk

The connection with the final exhibition may remain implicit for pupils.

### Required adjustment during delivery

At the end of the class recap, explicitly add:

> This sentence can help us introduce why our future exhibition matters.

Do not expand the final task yet. Just make the transfer visible.

## 2. Cognitive load

### Diagnosis

The intended charge is controlled:
- reduced timeline;
- 3 or 4 key elements;
- simple categories;
- one reusable sentence.

### Main risk

The timeline may contain too many dates, events or political references. If the teacher uses the full timeline, the lesson will exceed B1/B1+ cognitive load and become a history lecture.

### Required adjustment

Before class, select only 3 or 4 timeline items maximum.

Recommended selection logic:
- one item connected with wall / fence;
- one item connected with control;
- one item connected with movement / crossing;
- one item connected with community / dialogue / artistic response.

Do not ask pupils to memorise dates.

## 3. Access to meaning

### Diagnosis

The lesson uses suitable access-to-meaning operations:
- identify document type;
- spot;
- classify;
- associate fact and meaning;
- reformulate.

This is aligned with the vault principle that pupils must act on the document rather than answer a list of comprehension questions.

### Strength

The structure `Fact from the timeline → Category → Meaning` is effective. It makes the cognitive move visible: pupils do not only identify information; they interpret its relevance.

### Risk

The question “What does it suggest?” may be too open if pupils have not first classified the fact.

### Required adjustment

Keep the order strict:

```text
spot → classify → infer meaning → produce sentence
```

Do not ask for symbolic meaning before the fact has been placed in a category.

## 4. ZPD and scaffolding

### Diagnosis

The lesson is well calibrated for B1/B1+ if the support is reduced.

The progression is appropriate:

reactivate Lesson 1 → identify timeline → spot key facts → classify → infer → recap

### Strength

The task allows weaker pupils to contribute through key words and stronger pupils to justify or nuance.

### Risk

The structure not only... also... may be new or fragile for some pupils.

### Required adjustment

Model orally before production:

The border is not only a wall. It also tells a story.

Then expand:

It also tells a story of control, movement and human lives.

For weaker pupils, accept:

The border is not only a wall. It is also a story.

## 5. Status of the worksheet

### Diagnosis

The lesson can function without a worksheet.

The board table is enough if the timeline is projected clearly.

### Decision

A worksheet is optional, not necessary.

If a worksheet is produced, it must remain very light:

one reactivation line;
one timeline spotting table;
one fact → meaning table;
one sentence frame;
one recap space.

No date-memorisation exercise. No long paragraph. No comprehension questionnaire.

## 6. Class recap

### Diagnosis

The proposed recap is strong and reusable.

It includes:

physical dimension: wall;
political dimension: control;
human dimension: movement, people, communities;
transition toward art as expression.

### Risk

The recap contains several abstract terms: political, symbolic, expression. Some pupils may copy without understanding.

### Required adjustment

Build the recap in two layers.

Layer 1 — accessible version:

The border is not only a wall. It also tells a story of control, movement and people.

Layer 2 — enriched version:

It is also a political, human and symbolic space.

Only copy the enriched version if pupils have orally processed it.

## 8. Language targets

### Diagnosis

The language targets are coherent and limited.

Useful structures:

The timeline shows that...
The border is not only...
It also represents...
It can symbolize...
This helps us understand why artists...

### Risk

Too many structures may be expected in production.

### Required adjustment

For pupil production, keep only:

The border is not only...
It also tells a story of...

The other structures remain teacher-led scaffolding or recognition language.

## 9. Phonology

### Diagnosis

The phonology is light and useful.

The contrast WALL / STORY supports meaning. The segmentation of:

The border is not only a wall.
It also tells a story.

is useful for future EOC.

### Required adjustment

Keep this to a one-minute recap repetition. Do not create a separate phonology phase.

## 10. Evaluation observable

### Diagnosis

The formative indicators are appropriate:

recognise a timeline;
spot relevant elements;
classify;
infer meaning;
produce a useful sentence.

### Required adjustment

Do not collect written work. Observe 2 or 3 pupils who struggle with fact → meaning and support them in Lesson 3.

## 11. Digital use

### Diagnosis

Digital use is minimal and appropriate if the timeline is projected.

### Risk

A dense digital slide may overload pupils.

### Required adjustment

If projected, use visual cropping or highlighting. Do not show the full page if only 3 or 4 items are needed.

## 12. Corrections before implementation

Minor implementation corrections:

Select 3 or 4 timeline items before class.
Do not use the full timeline.
Do not ask pupils to memorise dates.
Maintain the order: spot → classify → infer → produce.
Model not only... also... before pupil production.
Keep the recap in two layers: simple first, enriched second.
Make the transfer to the final pitch explicit at the end.
